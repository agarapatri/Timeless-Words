Sāṅkhya Yoga - The Path of Knowledge

Chapter 2, Verse 47 of 72

Verse 47 of 72

Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥

IAST Transliteration

karmaṇy evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana
mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo 'stv akarmaṇi

Grammatical Analysis

कर्मणि (karmaṇi): Locative singular of कर्मन् (karman), neuter noun meaning "action" or "work"

अधिकारः (adhikāraḥ): Nominative singular masculine, compound with ते (te) forming possessive

मा फलेषु (mā phaleṣu): Prohibitive particle with locative plural of फल (phala)

English Translation

"You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but never to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty."

Śaṅkarācārya's Commentary

This verse establishes the fundamental principle of niṣkāma karma (desireless action). The right to action (adhikāra) belongs to the individual, but the results are governed by divine will and natural law. This teaching forms the cornerstone of karma yoga.

Philosophical Context

The verse addresses Arjuna's dilemma about action and inaction. Krishna teaches that complete withdrawal from action is neither possible nor desirable, but attachment to results causes suffering. The middle path is action without attachment.
